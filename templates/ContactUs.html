Computer Viruses : A computer virus is a malicious software program designed to infect a computer system, replicate itself, and spread to other devices. It typically attaches itself to legitimate programs or files and can be triggered when a user unknowingly runs the infected software. Once activated, a virus can cause various forms of harm, such as corrupting data, slowing down system performance, stealing personal information, or even rendering a system unusable. Viruses spread through email attachments, downloads, and compromised websites, making it crucial for users to maintain strong cybersecurity practices and use updated antivirus software to protect their devices.

How to Detect if Your System is Infected with a Virus : To determine if your system is infected with malware or a virus, look for signs such as unexpected slowdowns, frequent crashes, or system freezes. Unusual pop-up messages, new or unfamiliar programs starting up automatically, or changes to your homepage or browser settings can also be indicators. Additionally, if you notice a significant increase in data usage or suspicious activity on your network, it might point to an infection. Running a full system scan with a reputable antivirus or antimalware program is the most reliable way to detect infections.
