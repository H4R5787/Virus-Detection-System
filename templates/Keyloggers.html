    Keyloggers: The Silent Threat to Your Privacy

    In the world of cybersecurity, few tools are as stealthy and invasive as keyloggers. These malicious programs silently track every keystroke you make, capturing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, and personal messages. Whether used by cybercriminals, corporations, or even governments, keyloggers are a dangerous tool that can compromise your privacy and security without your knowledge. In this blog, we’ll delve into what keyloggers are, how they work, their potential impacts, and how to protect yourself against them.

    What is a Keylogger ?

    A keylogger, short for "keystroke logger," is a type of surveillance software or hardware that records every keystroke made on a computer or mobile device. The main goal of keyloggers is to collect information that can be used for unauthorized access to accounts, financial data, or personal information. Keyloggers can be software-based, hardware-based, or even installed as part of a larger malware attack.

    Types of Keyloggers

    Keyloggers can be categorized into two main types: software-based and hardware-based keyloggers.
    1. Software-Based Keyloggers:
    These keyloggers are malicious software programs that are installed on a device to record keystrokes. They operate within the operating system and are the most common type of keyloggers.
    Kernel-Level Keyloggers: These keyloggers run at the kernel level (the core of the operating system), making them difficult to detect and remove. They can access low-level system functions to record keystrokes.
    User-Level Keyloggers: These operate at the application level and are typically easier to detect by antivirus software. They may be part of a trojan or malware package.

    2. Hardware-Based Keyloggers:
    Hardware keyloggers are physical devices installed on the computer, often between the keyboard and the computer itself. These keyloggers don’t require software installation, making them more difficult to detect but easier to remove once found.
    USB Keyloggers: These are small devices inserted into a USB port between the keyboard and the computer. They capture the signals sent from the keyboard.
    Wireless Keyloggers: These keyloggers capture keystrokes from wireless keyboards by intercepting the signals transmitted between the keyboard and the receiver.

    Rootkits

    Rootkits are malicious software that allow unauthorized users to access and control a computer while remaining undetected by security measures.
